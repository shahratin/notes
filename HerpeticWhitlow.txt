Herpetic whitlow
	Diagnosis:

	Intensely painful infection of the
	fingers(usually terminal phalanx)
	by HSV-1 (40%), HSV-2 (60%)

	Concurrent herpes labialis or 
	gingivostomatitis

	Prodrome 2 - 20 days

	Erythema, oedema on erythematous
	base. Vesicles over 3 - 5 mm 
	area with cloudy or haemorrhagic 
	fluid.

	Lymphangitic streaking with
	epitrochlear or axillary 
	lymphadenopathy.

	Management:
	
	Virux cream 5%
	Apply 5 times for 5 days
	
	Tab. Virux 400 mg
	2 + 0 + 2 during prodrome
